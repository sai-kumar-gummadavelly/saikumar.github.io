Projects

P/01
Stock Market Trend Prediction via ML & Deep Learning — Continuous vs Binary Data
A rigorous comparative analysis of machine learning and deep learning applied to financial forecasting. Linear regression and SVMs for continuous data; logistic regression and decision trees for binary classification. LSTM and RNN architectures explored for temporal dependencies. Evaluated across accuracy, precision, recall, and F1 — building insight into both the promise and limits of algorithmic market prediction.
P/02
Expressive, Efficient & Revocable Data Access Control — Multi-Authority Cloud Storage · CP-ABE
Addressing a core challenge in cloud security: attribute revocation in Ciphertext-Policy Attribute-Based Encryption (CP-ABE). Designed a revocable multi-authority CP-ABE scheme enabling independent attribute issuance across multiple authorities while maintaining both forward and backward security. Data owners retain direct control over access policies even in untrusted cloud environments.

A Mind Driven by Logic

REASON

Atheism, for me, is not just a belief — it is a way of thinking. I see the world through logic, questions, and curiosity. Instead of accepting things without reason, I try to understand why things exist and how they work.

This mindset has shaped both my personal life and my journey in technology. It has taught me to rely on evidence, think critically, and stay open to new ideas. In a field like computer science and research, this approach helps me solve problems with clarity and make decisions based on facts rather than assumptions.

Being an atheist has also made me more responsible for my own actions. I believe that meaning in life is something we create through our work, our learning, and the way we treat others. It pushes me to stay grounded, focused, and driven by purpose.

In my journey — whether in research, engineering, or daily life — I carry this mindset: questioning, learning, and growing continuously.
